Bambarakanda Falls

Bambarakanda Falls is the tallest waterfall in Sri Lanka, plunging dramatically from a height of 263 meters (863 feet). Located in the Badulla District, near the village of Kalupahana, this spectacular fall is surrounded by dense pine forests and the misty hills of the central highlands. It is formed by the Kuda Oya, a tributary of the Walawe River, and is best visited during or shortly after the monsoon season when the water flow is at its most powerful.

Despite its grandeur, Bambarakanda maintains a tranquil atmosphere, making it a peaceful destination for hikers, nature lovers, and photographers. The trail to the falls is relatively easy, and visitors can enjoy a serene walk through forest paths and grassy hills. Its untouched natural surroundings and remote location give it a quiet charm, far from the bustle of more commercial tourist spots.

Diyaluma Falls

Diyaluma Falls, standing at 220 meters (720 feet), is the second-highest waterfall in Sri Lanka. It is located near Koslanda in the Badulla District and is one of the most iconic and photographed waterfalls in the country. The falls cascade down a steep rock face in a single vertical sheet before splashing into the rocks below, creating a stunning visual spectacle especially during the rainy season.

What sets Diyaluma apart is the series of natural rock pools found at the top of the falls. Many adventurous travelers hike to the top to swim in these pools and enjoy breathtaking views of the valley below. The blend of adventure, relaxation, and natural beauty makes Diyaluma a popular destination for backpackers and nature enthusiasts. However, care must be taken at the top, as the rocks can be slippery and the drop extremely steep.

Bopath Ella

Bopath Ella is a waterfall located in Kuruwita, in the Ratnapura District, and is named after its distinctive shape that resembles the leaf of the sacred Bo tree. Falling from a height of about 30 meters, Bopath Ella is not among the tallest waterfalls, but its unique shape and accessibility make it a favorite among locals and tourists alike. The water flows down in a wide spread that narrows at the base, resembling a teardrop leaf.

The area around Bopath Ella is rich in folklore and legend, with tales of tragic romances and ancient rituals said to be connected to the site. The waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery and small eateries, making it a comfortable and culturally rich spot for day-trippers from Colombo. It is also a popular destination for pilgrims visiting nearby temples.

Ravana Falls

Ravana Falls is a prominent waterfall located near Ella in the Uva Province. Cascading down from a height of about 25 meters, the waterfall is part of the Ravana Ella Wildlife Sanctuary. It is closely tied to the ancient Indian epic, the Ramayana, with legends claiming that King Ravana once hid Princess Sita in the nearby Ravana Cave, which adds a layer of mythological intrigue to the area.

Ravana Falls is easily visible from the Ella-Wellawaya main road and is a popular stop for tourists traveling in the region. During the rainy season, the waterfall swells and gushes down over a series of jagged rocks, creating an impressive sight. Visitors often enjoy a short trek to the base or take quick dips in the shallow natural pools formed by the cascading waters.

Lakshapana Falls

Lakshapana Falls is one of the most powerful and important waterfalls in Sri Lanka, both scenically and functionally. It drops from a height of approximately 126 meters and is located near Norton Bridge in the Nuwara Eliya District. The waterfall is formed by the Maskeliya Oya, a tributary of the Kelani River, and plays a vital role in the country’s hydroelectric power system, feeding several power stations in the area.

Surrounded by tea plantations and hilly terrain, Lakshapana is also a sight to behold. The name "Lakshapana" is believed to mean "a hundred thousand lamps" - possibly a reference to its power and brilliance. Though slightly off the main tourist trail, it offers a magnificent view and a peaceful atmosphere for those willing to make the journey. The trek to the falls also provides excellent photo opportunities of the countryside.

Ramboda Falls

Ramboda Falls is a multi-tiered waterfall located in the Pussellawa area of the Nuwara Eliya District. Standing at around 109 meters high, it is the 11th tallest waterfall in the country and is fed by the Panna Oya, a tributary of the Kotmale River. The surrounding area is famous for its scenic beauty, with tea plantations, cool climate, and lush forests making it a delightful stop for travelers.

The falls are easily accessible from the Kandy-Nuwara Eliya main road, and a number of hotels and viewing platforms have been established nearby. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the cascade from above or descend to the base for a more immersive experience. The combination of altitude, greenery, and the sound of rushing water makes Ramboda Falls a memorable part of any trip through the hill country.

Dunhinda Falls

Dunhinda Falls is considered one of the most beautiful waterfalls in Sri Lanka. Located near the city of Badulla, it falls gracefully from a height of 64 meters (210 feet) into a large pool surrounded by dense forest. The waterfall gets its name from the Sinhala word "dun" meaning mist or smoke, referring to the fine spray that rises from the base and gives the area a mystical appearance.

A 1.5 km trek through forest trails leads to the waterfall, offering a peaceful walk rich in bird calls and lush greenery. Dunhinda is not just a scenic wonder but also holds a place in local lore and history, having once been a site of ancient settlements. The view of the single stream plunging from a narrow cleft into a wide pool below is captivating and remains etched in the memory of anyone who visits.

Devon Falls

Devon Falls is located in the Nuwara Eliya District near the town of Talawakele. It is named after an English coffee planter named Devon who lived in the area during colonial times. Standing at a height of 97 meters (318 feet), the waterfall is often referred to as the "Veil of the Valley" due to its graceful and elegant appearance.

The fall consists of three distinct tiers, and it can be viewed clearly from the main road and several roadside cafes, making it easily accessible to travelers. Set against a backdrop of rolling tea estates and mist-covered hills, Devon Falls is especially beautiful during the rainy season when the flow is more powerful. Its romantic charm and historic association make it a favorite stop for couples and nature lovers alike.

Baker's Falls

Baker's Falls is a charming waterfall located inside Horton Plains National Park in the Nuwara Eliya District. It is named after the British explorer Sir Samuel Baker. The waterfall, though only 20 meters high, is wide and strong, with water rushing over a rock face framed by thick vegetation and mist. The area is part of a high-altitude ecosystem, giving it a cool, fresh climate year-round.

To reach Baker’s Falls, visitors must undertake a moderate trek of about 2 km from the Horton Plains trailhead, passing scenic landscapes and possibly encountering wildlife. The fall is most impressive during the wet season, when its force is at its peak. The surrounding flora, including rhododendrons and ferns, enhance its beauty, making it one of the most visited attractions within the park.

Lover's Leap Falls

Lover's Leap Falls is a charming and romantic waterfall located just a few kilometers from the heart of Nuwara Eliya, Sri Lanka. Dropping from a height of around 30 meters, the waterfall flows gracefully down a rocky cliff surrounded by lush greenery and tea plantations. It is easily accessible through a short but scenic hike starting from the Pedro Tea Estate, making it a popular stop for nature enthusiasts and those exploring the cool, misty landscapes of the hill country.

The name of the waterfall comes from a tragic local legend in which a prince and a village girl, whose love was forbidden, leapt from the cliff to their deaths, choosing to be together in the afterlife. This romantic story has given Lover's Leap Falls a reputation as a symbol of eternal love and sacrifice. Visitors are often captivated not just by the natural beauty of the falls, but also by the emotional depth of the legend tied to it.
